     `/en/blog/nitro-type-alternative/` — kid + parent, competitor-brand capture.

letter promotion, accuracy-gated pacing) — and a parent-facing proof-of-learning. That is a
genuine, non-duplicative position, not a keyword trick (guardrail 7 / SEO.md §9).

## 2. SERP evidence (dated, no invented volumes)

Every claim below is from live web results on **2026-07-23**. These establish *intent and
competition character*, which is what we prioritise on in the absence of a volume tool.

- **`typing for kids` / `learn to type for kids`** — a **mixed** SERP: free courses
  (Typing.com, TypingClub/edclub "Jungle Junior", KidzType typing-web), game portals
  (TurtleDiary, TypingGames.Zone, TypeKids), and the BBC Dance Mat legacy via third-party
  guides. No single "free + fun + teaches-from-zero + no ads" hub owns it. This is the pillar
  target: broad, competitive, mixed — a topical anchor, not a quick win.
- **`free typing games for kids`** — a **listicle-dominated** SERP: Today's Parent
  ("10 Free Typing Games for Kids"), Connections Academy ("5 Free Typing Games"), Class Tech
  Tips, KidzType, plus game portals. This is the highest-value *joinable* query: an honest
  "best free typing games" guide is a legitimate page (nl already ships the exact analogue,
  `beste-gratis-typspelletjes-kinderen`) and Typcoon is a defensible entry for the
  free+fun+teaches-from-zero niche — ranked on merit, not by out-muscling the portals.
- **`what age should kids learn to type` / `best age to learn typing`** — a **Q&A / blog**
  SERP: Learning.com, Ratatype, Typing.com's own blog, SheKnows, Education World, OT blogs.
  Informational, parent research-stage, **attainable** with a genuinely useful expert-framed
  answer (the consensus the SERP itself yields: introduce keys ~5-6, systematic ~7-9, full
  touch typing ~grade 4 / age ~10). nl ships the analogue `op-welke-leeftijd-leren-typen`.
- **`nitro type alternative`** — competitor-brand intent. Nitro Type is positioned across the
  results as fun *racing practice for kids who already type*, which is exactly the honest
  contrast: Nitro Type sharpens speed; Typcoon teaches the technique from zero. nl ships the

- **`touch typing for kids` / `how to touch type`** — method-specific parent intent
  (KidzType, TypingClub, KAZ, EnglishType, Love Learning Tutors round-ups). Higher-qualified
  but lower-volume than the game terms; folded into the pillar and a fast-follow spoke, not a

## 3. Native intent — proof these are NOT translated Dutch keywords

SEO.md §5 forbids machine-translating the nl keyword set. The check: does the en target come
from the *English* SERP's own phrasing and its *own* competitor brands, or is it a literal
render of a Dutch term? Side-by-side:

| Concept | Dutch target (SEO.md §2, in-market) | A naive *translation* would give | The **native English** target we chose | Why native ≠ translation |
|--------|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Pillar / head | `leren typen voor kinderen` | "learn to type for children" | **`typing for kids`** / `learn to type for kids` | English uses "kids" not "children" in this query, and "typing for kids" is the phrase the SERP actually clusters on; competitors are TypingClub/Typing.com, **not** TypeTopia/Typetuin. |
| Fun/free games | `typspelletjes` / `gratis typspel` | "typing little-games" (no natural EN render) | **`free typing games for kids`** | "typspelletjes" has no English equivalent as a word; the native query is the full "free typing games for kids" listicle phrase. |
| Age/readiness | `op welke leeftijd leren typen` | "at what age learn typing" | **`what age should kids learn to type`** | Native EN phrasing + the EN SERP frames it around US school grades (grade 2-4), not Dutch groepen 6-8. |
| Competitor capture | `nitro type` (also a brand in NL) | (same brand) | **`nitro type alternative`** | Brand overlaps, but the EN framing and audience (kids who race) is derived from the EN SERP, and pairs against `free typing games for kids`. |
| Dutch-only, **dropped** | `typediploma`, `leren typen groep 6/7/8` | — | *(not targeted)* | These are **culture-specific Dutch intents** (the "typediploma" expectation, Dutch primary-school groepen). There is **no English demand** for them — proof we did not translate the nl list wholesale. English equivalents (US grade levels, "typing certificate") are weaker intents and are **not** launch targets. |

The dropped-row is the strongest evidence of native derivation: two of nl's highest-value
terms (`typediploma`, `groep 6/7/8`) have **no en counterpart worth a page**, and the en set
introduces a term (`free typing games for kids` as a listicle) whose Dutch analogue exists but
under different competitive dynamics. The sets overlap in *concept* where the concept is
universal (age, competitor capture) and **diverge in phrasing, competitor brand, and which
intents even exist** — which is exactly what "native, not translated" means.

## 5. Attainability & method (SEO.md §7 caveat honoured)

**No keyword tool is available in this environment.** I cannot pull real search volumes
(Ahrefs / Semrush / Google Keyword Planner are not accessible here), and per SEO.md §7 and §9
I will **not** invent numbers. The method used instead, identical to how nl was prioritised:

**Priority = intent × attainability**, scored from the dated SERP evidence in §2:

- **Intent fit** — does the query match a buyer (parent) or player (kid) the charter wants,
  and does our product honestly answer it? (All four targets: yes.)
- **Attainability** — read from SERP character: a **listicle** SERP (spoke 1) or a **Q&A/blog**
  SERP (spoke 2) is joinable by a genuinely-useful page; a **competitor-brand** SERP (spoke 3)
  rewards the honest alternative; a **broad mixed** SERP (pillar) is a long compounding play,
  not a quick win.

**The honest en-specific caveat (this is the load-bearing warning):** English is *not* nl's
home-field. TypingClub and Typing.com are global incumbents with domain authority typcoon
cannot match at launch. So:

1. The **pillar** ranks slowly and on topical-authority accrual — treat it as the anchor, not
   a week-2 win (SEO.md §9: "don't judge week 2").
2. The **launch spokes are deliberately the attainable long-tail / informational / capture
   terms**, mirroring how nl won its long-tail before the head term. Do not gate launch on
   ranking the head term.
3. **Before the en blog scales past these launch spokes, get a real volume tool** (SEO.md §7
   is explicit) and re-rank — the same way `content-batch-2-scope.md` §4 says 006's data
   re-ranks nl batch 3. Flagged in §6.
