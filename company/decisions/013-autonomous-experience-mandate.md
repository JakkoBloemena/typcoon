# ADR 013 — Standing mandate: iterate autonomously toward the ultimate game experience

- **Date:** 2026-07-24
- **Decided by:** Shareholder (direct, /ceo channel). Verbatim: "please keep
  building without my approvals. Just spend iterations on making the whole game
  the ultimate experience."

## Decision

1. **Product and design decisions no longer wait for Shareholder approval.** The
   designer, PO, and CEO decide and ship within the charter's guardrails. The
   Shareholder sees results (Telegram, /ceo, artifacts), and their direction —
   whenever given — remains the highest-priority input; it is simply no longer a
   *gate*.
2. **The experience flywheel is the standing work supply.** The loop must never
   again conclude "the plan is done, hold" for the product experience (the ADR 010
   mistake, already corrected by 011). The cycle is permanent: **playtest-critique
   (as a kid, as a parent: is this the ultimate version of this moment?) → design
   iteration → build → verify → playtest again.** Each critique's findings are the
   next iteration's backlog — the PO scopes them, the designer deepens them,
   developers build them. 076 and every future playtest gate are re-purposed
   accordingly: not pass/fail gates but the flywheel's intake.
3. **"Ultimate experience" is the bar, judged honestly.** Critique lanes must
   compare against the best of the genre a kid could be playing instead, and say
   plainly where typcoon feels basic, flat, or dutiful. Flattery in a critique is
   a defect.

## What still comes to the Shareholder (unchanged, charter reporting rules)

Real-money spend or anything above the €50/mo ceiling; legal/reputational
exposure; pivots from the thesis; the payments/entity decision when trigger 010
fires. Everything else: build, ship, iterate.

## Consequences

- After the current milestone (079 world pass → PO cut → build → 076 critique),
  the flywheel self-seeds: 076's findings become the next iteration's assignments
  without waiting for a /ceo session.
- Guardrails are the only hard walls (kids' product: no ads, no selling speed, no
  child-completable purchases, privacy-matches-code, free tier real, no SEO
  tricks). A critique finding that collides with a guardrail escalates to the CEO
  instead of shipping.
- The CEO's weekly Shareholder report continues; it reports what shipped and what
  the flywheel found, not what awaits permission.
