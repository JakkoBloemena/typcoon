A typing game, not a typing course

Most programs for learning to type are a course with a game bolted on as a reward. Typcoon flips that around: it is a tycoon game — the kind kids already spend hours building and optimizing — where typing is the only way to make progress. No auto-clickers, no shortcuts: whoever types neatly, and gets steadily faster, builds the biggest factory.

For parents: real learning happens here

  • Adaptive curriculum — letters are introduced one at a time, exactly when your kid is ready.
  • Accuracy first — sloppy mashing earns next to nothing; typing neatly is the big lever. Speed follows naturally after.
  • Smart repetition — weak letters and letter combinations come back more often (spaced repetition), so they stick.
  • Instant feedback — on every keystroke, every task. So bad habits never get a chance to set in.
  • Privacy-friendly — plays entirely without an account; no third-party tracking, no cookies, no ads — only anonymous, non-identifying usage statistics. All progress stays local. An optional parent account (email only) enables sync and a weekly progress email.

Frequently asked questions

Is Typcoon free?

You play for free: the first letters (the home row) and your first machines, no account needed. For the full alphabet and the complete factory there's one one-time family unlock — no subscription, no ads, and never a purchase your child can make on their own.

Does my kid actually learn to touch type here?

Yes. Under the hood runs an adaptive learning engine: letters are introduced step by step, weak letters get smart repetition, and accuracy comes before speed. Typing is the only way to earn coins.

What age is it for?

Built for kids roughly 8 to 12 years old — but anyone who wants to learn touch typing can play.

Can it be played on a tablet?

Typcoon is played with a real keyboard, so on a laptop or computer — that's how your kid learns correct finger placement.
